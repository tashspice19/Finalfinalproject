The Justice System


Since I was a child, I have always been inspired and have found deep interest in African American history and race relations. During my junior year I wanted to educate myself more on the subject so I began reading texts from “The Color Purple” To the “New Jim Crow” by Michelle Alexander, constantly updating myself through the news, and seeing speakers like Ta Nehisi Coates talk. My exploration in the subject has broadened my understanding of the corrupt prison and justice system we have; mandatory minimum sentencing and overcharging which incentive plea deals lead to innocent people confessing to crimes they did not commit.The militarization of police and targeting of ethnic minorities have lead to the loss of life and growth of tension. Prisons for profit encourage substandard conditions for inmates and instead of fostering rehabilitation, a cycle of reincarnation continues. I could go on with the list of shocking, gross realities of our justice system in America but what I find to be more informative is the articles and news sites that helped educate me on the subject in the first place.
